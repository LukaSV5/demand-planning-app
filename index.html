The seven-model lineup Seasonal Naive → Global XGBoost

Different products behave differently — some follow strong seasonal cycles, others respond to market signals, and many are too noisy for a single algorithm. Rather than force one model on everything, the workspace runs a seven-model lineup and lets each SKU pick its own winner.

  • Seasonal Naive — the benchmark every other model must beat (MASE is scored against it).
  • SES / EMA — exponential smoothing for level-dominant, trendless demand.
  • Theta — the M3-competition workhorse; robust decomposition + drift.
  • Auto-ETS — damped-trend Holt-Winters, smoothing auto-tuned per SKU. Usually the flagship.
  • Prophet-style — trend + Fourier seasonal harmonics; stable on short, messy histories.
  • Global XGBoost — one gradient-boosted model across all SKUs, so sparse products borrow strength portfolio-wide.
  • Top-3 Ensemble — blends the three best validated models when no single one dominates.

Best Fit evaluates every model on each SKU and routes it to the lowest-error winner — automatically, no manual tuning.

How accuracy is measured WAPE · MASE · walk-forward

The headline number is walk-forward (rolling-origin) validation, not in-sample fit. The model is trained on history up to a point, asked to forecast forward, and scored only on months it never saw — repeated across multiple origins so there's zero leakage from the future.

  • WAPE — weighted absolute % error; the primary ranking metric.
  • MASE — error relative to seasonal naive (< 1.0 means it genuinely beats the benchmark).
  • MAE / RMSE — reported alongside for scale and outlier sensitivity.
  • 80% prediction intervals — empirical, derived from the walk-forward residuals; this is where safety stock lives.

In-sample fit is shown too — deliberately, so you can see how optimistic it is next to the number you can actually plan on.

What runs in your browser Web Worker · IndexedDB · zero server

The entire engine is client-side — your data never leaves the tab. A background Web Worker backtests all seven models on every SKU off the main thread, so the UI never freezes; results are cached and the loaded dataset persists in IndexedDB, so a reload restores instantly instead of recomputing.

  • Per-SKU auto-routing streams winners into the table as they're scored.
  • Forecast cache keyed to the dataset — reloads skip the re-sweep.
  • The replenishment table is virtualized to stay smooth at 10k+ SKUs.
  • No backend, no signup, no telemetry — just open and run.
Evidence for every claim Accuracy · Intervals · Routing · Replenishment

Everything below is live in the workspace — nothing on a roadmap, nothing "coming soon."

  • Honest accuracy — Every model is re-trained from scratch on an expanding window, then scored only on months it never saw. WAPE and MASE are pooled across all origins and horizons with zero future leakage. In-sample fit is shown alongside but explicitly demoted.
  • 7 models, 1 winner per SKU — A Web Worker backtests Seasonal Naive through Global XGBoost on every SKU and routes each to its proven winner. A progress chip counts SKUs as they complete; a leaderboard shows exactly why the winner won.
  • Uncertainty quantified — Every forecast ships with an 80% band built from real walk-forward residuals — empirical 10th/90th percentiles, lower always ≥ 0 and ≤ forecast ≤ upper. Not a decorative cone.
  • Portfolio learning — One gradient-boosted model per horizon, trained across your whole catalog. Sparse SKUs borrow strength from portfolio-wide patterns; scale-normalized per SKU.
  • Beat the naive forecast, or say so — MASE < 1 means it earned its complexity. MASE ≥ 1 shows up red — the app says that to your face, and Best Fit routes around it.
  • From prediction to purchase order — Reorder point = safety stock + lead-time demand. To-Order rounds to MOQ. Cover days, health status, and projected replenish date on every row.
From CSV to defensible order quantity 01 Connect → 02 Audit → 03 Decide → 04 Replenish

No onboarding call. No implementation project. The demo dataset loads in one click if you'd rather not bring your own.

  • 01 · Connect — Drop in your CSVs: sales history, stock levels, suppliers, purchase orders. Columns are auto-mapped and every assignment is re-mappable. Or press Load demo dataset and skip straight to step 2.
  • 02 · Audit — A Web Worker walk-forward-validates all seven models on every SKU in the background while you browse. A progress chip streams results; winners are cached so drawers open instantly on reload.
  • 03 · Decide — The routed model is pre-selected. Switch between all 7, override via the leaderboard, read the 80% band, stretch the history horizon, export PNG for your S&OP deck.
  • 04 · Replenish — The replenishment workspace prices every row off the winning model's next-3-month forecast: To-Order at MOQ, reorder points, cover days, health status, projected replenish dates.

Headline metrics: WAPE and MASE under walk-forward (rolling-origin) validation — re-fit per origin, errors pooled across origins × horizon, MASE benchmarked against seasonal naive.
